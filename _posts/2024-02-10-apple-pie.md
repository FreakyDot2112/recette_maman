title: "Classic Apple Pie"
description: "Flaky butter crust and cinnamon-spiced filling. The smell alone will bring everyone to the kitchen."
category: "Desserts"
emoji: "🥧"
date: 2024-02-10
prep_time: "40 min"
cook_time: "55 min"
servings: 8
difficulty: Medium
ingredients:
  - "For the crust:"
  - 2½ cups all-purpose flour
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 1 tbsp sugar
  - 1 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  - 6–8 tbsp ice cold water
  - "For the filling:"
  - 6 large apples (Granny Smith or mix)
  - ¾ cup sugar
  - 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  - 1 tsp cinnamon
  - ¼ tsp nutmeg
  - 1 tbsp lemon juice
  - 2 tbsp butter, cut into pieces
  - 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
steps:
  - Make the crust — whisk together flour, salt, and sugar. Cut in the cold butter until pea-sized crumbs form. Add ice water one tablespoon at a time until the dough just comes together. Divide in two, flatten into disks, wrap, and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  - Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Peel, core, and slice the apples into ¼-inch slices. Toss with sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Let sit 15 minutes.
  - Roll out one dough disk on a floured surface into a 12-inch circle. Drape into a 9-inch pie dish and trim the edges.
  - Pour the apple filling in, mounding slightly in the center. Dot with butter pieces. Roll out the second dough disk and lay over the top. Trim and crimp the edges together. Cut a few slits in the top to vent.
  - Brush the top with beaten egg. Sprinkle with a little sugar. Bake at 425°F for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F and bake another 35–40 minutes until golden brown and bubbling.
  - Let cool at least 2 hours before slicing — the filling needs time to set. Serve with vanilla ice cream.
notes: "The colder your butter, the flakier your crust. Mom kept a stick of butter in the freezer just for pie. Also: don't skip letting the pie cool — slicing too soon is the only real mistake here."
